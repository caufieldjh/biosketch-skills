# NIH Biosketch Requirements Checklist

Based on the new NIH biosketch format effective for applications with due dates
on or after January 25, 2026. Biosketches using the older format should be
flagged for updating.

## New format overview

The new NIH biosketch (effective 01/25/2026, with leniency through May 2026 per
NOT-OD-26-033) consists of two simultaneous components that generate a single PDF:

1. **Biosketch Common Form** — A standardized federal format used across agencies.
   Contains: Professional Preparation, Appointments & Positions, Products.
2. **NIH Biographical Sketch Supplement** — Agency-specific sections.
   Contains: Personal Statement, Honors, Contributions to Science.

This restructuring was mandated by the Office of Science and Technology to align
disclosure processes across federal research agencies.

### Key structural changes from old format
- Citations are consolidated into a dedicated Products section (no longer
  embedded throughout narrative sections)
- Character limits replace page-based limits for individual sections (SciENcv
  enforces these — it will not save a section that exceeds its character limit)
- SciENcv is mandatory — only the investigator (not delegates) may certify
- ORCID iD is required and must be linked to eRA Commons
- After February 6, 2026, submission errors block applications that don't use
  the correct forms

## General format requirements

- [ ] Total biosketch does not exceed 5 pages
- [ ] No diagrams, figures, tables, or graphics (except the education table)
- [ ] Follows NIH font, size, and margin guidelines
- [ ] Prepared using SciENcv (required for new format)
- [ ] Certification statement present on final page (SciENcv adds this
      automatically when the investigator certifies the biosketch)

## Identifying information

- [ ] Full name present (Last, First, Middle, Suffix)
- [ ] ORCID iD present and linked to eRA Commons (required in new format)
- [ ] eRA Commons username present
- [ ] Current position title present
- [ ] Primary organization name and location present

### Old format indicators to flag
- Missing ORCID iD (was not previously required)
- Missing certification statement on final page

### Common SciENcv issues to note
- Some users have multiple NCBI accounts (one linked to ORCID, another to eRA
  Commons) — this can cause profile and display problems
- Delegates cannot edit profile sections — only the account holder can
- The platform may not display ORCID iD or eRA Commons username in profiles;
  users should click EDIT if the profile shows incomplete status

## Education/Training (Professional Preparation)

- [ ] Listed in reverse chronological order by start date
- [ ] Includes baccalaureate degree or initial professional education
- [ ] Postdoctoral and fellowship training listed separately
- [ ] Each entry includes: institution name, location, degree, dates, field of study
- [ ] Residency training included if applicable

## Personal Statement (NIH Supplement)

- [ ] Present and substantive
- [ ] Limited to 3,500 characters including spaces (new format — SciENcv
      enforces this limit and will not save content that exceeds it)
- [ ] Written in first person
- [ ] Explains why the individual is suited for the specific role
- [ ] Connects training and experience to the proposed work
- [ ] **No citations** (new format — may reference Products section instead,
      but no inline citations or citation lists)
- [ ] May highlight ongoing/completed projects from past three years
- [ ] Addresses any gaps in productivity if present (family circumstances, etc.)

### Old format indicators to flag
- Citations present in personal statement (no longer permitted — this was
  previously allowed with up to 4 citations)
- Personal statement exceeds 3,500 characters

### Quality indicators
- Does it clearly state the individual's role in the proposed project?
- Does it connect prior experience to the proposed work?
- Does it explain what makes this individual uniquely qualified?
- For fellowship applications: Does it explain how the research provides
  optimal training for career advancement?

## Appointments and Positions (Common Form)

- [ ] Listed in reverse chronological order
- [ ] Begins with current appointment
- [ ] Limited to academic/professional positions from past 3 years only —
      specifically positions outside the primary institution (new format)
- [ ] Each entry includes: start/end years, title, organization, department, location
- [ ] Includes domestic and foreign positions (paid or unpaid)

### Honors (NIH Supplement)
- [ ] Limited to 15 entries (new format)
- [ ] Includes relevant scholarships, fellowships, development awards
- [ ] For clinicians: includes clinical licensures and specialty board certifications

### Old format indicators to flag
- More than 15 honors listed
- External appointments older than 3 years
- Three-subsection structure (Positions and Employment / Other Experience and
  Professional Memberships / Honors) — this was the old format layout. The new
  format separates Appointments (Common Form) from Honors (Supplement).

## Contributions to Science (NIH Supplement)

- [ ] Up to 5 contributions maximum
- [ ] Each contribution limited to 2,000 characters (new format — SciENcv
      enforces this limit)
- [ ] Each contribution has a narrative description
- [ ] Narrative describes: historical background, central findings, influence on
      the field, and the individual's specific role
- [ ] **No citations within contribution narratives** (new format — narratives
      may cross-reference products from the Products section but cannot include
      formal citation lists)
- [ ] No figures, tables, or graphics

### Products section (Common Form)
- [ ] Up to 10 total products
- [ ] Up to 5 products most closely related to the proposed project
- [ ] Up to 5 other significant products demonstrating scientific contribution
- [ ] These become the *only* citable references for narrative sections
- [ ] Full citation information for each (authors, title, date, URL, identifiers)
- [ ] Includes persistent identifiers where available (DOI, PMID)
- [ ] PubMed-generated citations preferred (retain hyperlinks automatically)
- [ ] Full bibliography URL included (ORCID or PubMed citations only — My NCBI
      My Bibliography hyperlinks are **not** permitted in the Common Form)

### Old format: citations within contributions
- Up to 4 citations per contribution (old format embedded citations directly
  under each contribution narrative)

### Old format indicators to flag
- Formal citation lists under each contribution (old format structure —
  citations should now be in the separate Products section)
- More than 10 total products across both groups
- Each contribution exceeding 2,000 characters
- My Bibliography hyperlink used (not permitted in Common Form)

### Quality indicators
- Does each contribution tell a coherent story with an arc (problem -> gap ->
  action -> result -> impact)?
- Are the publications directly relevant to the contribution being described?
- Is the individual's specific role in the work clearly stated?
- Are the contributions strategically selected to demonstrate qualifications
  for the proposed work?

### Acceptable product types
Publications, conference papers, presentations, websites, technologies,
techniques, inventions, patents, patent applications, licenses, data, databases,
datasets, physical collections, audio/video products, software, models,
educational materials, instruments, equipment, research materials,
clinical/educational interventions, business creation

## Section D: Research Support (Optional)

- [ ] Lists ongoing and recently completed projects (past 3 years)
- [ ] Includes both federal and non-federal funding
- [ ] Each entry briefly indicates project goals and the individual's role
- [ ] Does NOT include person-months or effort percentages
- [ ] Ordered by relevance to the current proposal
- [ ] Only includes funding where the individual is named as key personnel
      (PI or Co-PI)

### Quality indicators
- Does the listed support demonstrate a track record of funded research?
- Is the support relevant to the proposed project?
- Are the descriptions brief and focused on goals, not methods?

## Certification (new format)

The new format requires investigators to certify their biosketch through SciENcv.
The certification statement appears on the final page and attests that:
- Information is current and accurate
- The individual is not part of a Malign Foreign Talent Recruitment Program
- Required research security training has been completed

Only the investigator can certify — delegates cannot do this on their behalf.
After certification, a certified PDF is downloaded for upload to the submission
portal (S2S, ASSIST, or Grants.gov).

## Common mistakes

1. **Exceeding page limit** — 5 pages maximum is strictly enforced
2. **Generic personal statement** — Not tailored to the specific proposal
3. **Citations in personal statement** — No longer permitted in new format
4. **Citations in contribution narratives** — No longer permitted; cross-reference
   Products section instead
5. **Listing all publications** — Should be strategically curated, not exhaustive
6. **Weak contribution narratives** — Lists of accomplishments instead of
   coherent stories about impact
7. **Missing ORCID iD** — Now required and must be linked to eRA Commons
8. **Outdated appointments** — External appointments should be within past 3 years
9. **Including figures or diagrams** — Not permitted
10. **Not bolding own name** — Best practice for reviewer visibility
11. **Using My Bibliography hyperlink** — Not permitted in Common Form; use
    ORCID or PubMed citation links instead
12. **Missing certification** — Biosketch must be certified by the investigator
    through SciENcv
13. **Delegate editing profile data** — Only the account holder can edit
    profile sections in SciENcv
14. **Not using SciENcv** — Mandatory for the new format; after Feb 6, 2026,
    submission errors block non-compliant applications
